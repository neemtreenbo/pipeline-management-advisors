# Deal Pipeline Stages — Sales Rep CRM

The Pipeline represents the progression of a **sales opportunity (Deal)** from initial identification to final outcome.  
Each stage reflects a meaningful step in the advisory process.

Pipeline Stages:

Opportunity
Contacted
Engaged
Schedule To Present
Proposal Presented
Decision Pending
Closed

---

## 1. Opportunity

This stage represents the creation of a potential sales opportunity.

An advisor has identified a person who may benefit from a financial or insurance solution. At this point, the advisor has not yet started meaningful outreach.

Typical sources include:
- Client referrals
- Personal network
- Event connections
- Social media contacts
- Existing clients with new financial needs

Goal of this stage:
Identify the opportunity and prepare for initial outreach.

Typical actions:
- Add contact information
- Identify possible insurance needs
- Plan first contact

---

## 2. Contacted

The advisor has initiated communication with the prospect.

Examples of contact attempts include:
- Phone calls
- WhatsApp or Messenger messages
- Email introduction
- Personal introduction through a referral

At this stage the advisor is attempting to open a conversation but engagement may still be limited.

Goal of this stage:
Establish a conversation with the prospect.

Possible outcomes:
- Prospect responds positively → move to Engaged
- Prospect declines → move to Closed
- Prospect does not respond → follow-up attempts

---

## 3. Engaged

The prospect is now actively interacting with the advisor.

Indicators of engagement include:
- Responding to messages
- Asking questions about insurance or financial planning
- Expressing curiosity or interest
- Open to discussing their financial situation

However, a formal meeting or presentation has not yet been scheduled.

Goal of this stage:
Secure a meeting where a proper consultation or presentation can occur.

Typical actions:
- Offer financial planning consultation
- Send calendar booking link
- Suggest possible meeting times

---

## 4. Schedule To Present

A meeting has been confirmed where the advisor will present a proposal or financial plan.

Examples:
- Zoom consultation
- Coffee meeting
- Financial planning session
- Client office meeting

Preparation typically happens in this stage.

Typical preparation activities:
- Conduct fact finding
- Prepare financial analysis
- Run insurance illustrations
- Build a proposal

Goal of this stage:
Prepare and schedule the proposal presentation.

---

## 5. Proposal Presented

The advisor has presented the insurance solution to the client.

This may include:
- Explaining the insurance plan
- Reviewing coverage and benefits
- Showing policy illustrations
- Uploading or sharing the proposal document

At this stage the client understands the recommended solution.

Goal of this stage:
Ensure the client clearly understands the proposal.

Typical activities:
- Clarifying questions
- Adjusting plan options
- Explaining riders or benefits
- Uploading proposal documents to the deal

---

## 6. Decision Pending

The client is currently evaluating the proposal.

Common scenarios include:
- Client reviewing the plan
- Discussing with spouse or family
- Comparing financial options
- Considering affordability

The advisor's role is primarily follow-up and support.

Typical activities:
- Follow-up communication
- Clarifying additional questions
- Revising proposal if needed

Goal of this stage:
Help the client reach a confident decision.

---

## 7. Closed

The opportunity has reached its final outcome.

Two possible outcomes:

Closed Won
The client proceeds with the insurance policy.

Closed Lost
The client declines or postpones the opportunity.

When closed, important data may include:
- Policy type
- Premium amount
- Policy number
- Closing date
- Reason for loss (if applicable)

This stage marks the completion of the deal lifecycle.
